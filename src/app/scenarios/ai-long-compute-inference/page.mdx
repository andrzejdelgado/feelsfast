# AI · Long compute / Inference


The user submits a request that is going to take 30 seconds or more. Image generation. A complex analytical query. A long-context reasoning task. The user has crossed the unit-task boundary; static skeletons no longer carry their weight; engagement is the last move before they leave.

This scenario sits in the 10 S+ band. Block & Zakay 1997's <Cite id="block-zakay-1997" label="Block & Zakay 1997" /> meta-analysis frames the trade-off — engagement compresses prospective duration while expanding retrospective duration; the design decision is whether you have made that trade deliberately. Fitch's <Cite id="fitch" label="Fitch" /> Slack and FIFA examples are the canonical references; Myers 1985 <Cite id="myers-1985" label="Myers 1985" /> is the determinate-progress fallback where the inference reports phases.

## What is happening

The ai-streaming demo stands in. For a real long inference, the tuned flow stacks more layers:

1. **Thinking state** during the time-to-first-token gap — the same dots-and-cursor pattern from [ai-chat-streaming-response](/scenarios/ai-chat-streaming-response).
2. **Streaming render** as soon as the first token arrives, paced to a natural reading rhythm.
3. **Tool-call transparency** if the inference involves visible tool calls — narrate them ("Searching…", "Reading…", "Reasoning…").
4. **Determinate progress** if the inference can report phases ("Step 3 of 7"); fall back to engagement otherwise.
5. **Cancellation always available** — the stop button must respond inside the perceptual frame even if the abort takes longer.
6. **Background fallback** — past 30–60 seconds, offer "do this in the background and notify me" so the user can leave the surface.

## What to tune

1. **Pre-action** — submit button echo within ~50 ms; thinking dots cover the time-to-first-token gap.
2. **First 1 s** — thinking state in place. No spinner, no skeleton over content the model will produce.
3. **1 – 10 s** — token streaming where text is the output. Tool-call transparency where the work is visible.
4. **Past 10 s** — engaging copy where applicable; determinate progress where the inference reports phases. Cancellation always visible.
5. **Past 30–60 s** — hand-off to background sync with notification. The foreground is no longer the right surface.

## When perceived performance hurts you here

The engagement-vs-retrospective-duration trade is the central trap. A 30-second inference with rich engagement feels short while it runs and long in retrospect — the user remembers it as taking forever even when their session went smoothly. Slack and FIFA accept this; for inference where the user repeats the action many times in a session, the retrospective cost compounds.

The cleaner answer for repeat-use AI inference: ship determinate progress where measurable, [tool-call transparency](/glossary#tool-call-transparency) where applicable (the user is *learning* during the wait), and background sync past 60 s. Generic engaging content (motivational quotes, mini-games) belongs only on rare or one-off inferences.

## Accessibility

1. **`aria-live="polite"`** on the streaming output and on tool-call narration.
2. **`aria-busy="true"`** during the inference; flip on completion.
3. **`prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`** — replace cross-fades and pulse animations with static states.
4. **Always provide a way out** — visible cancellation, visible "do in background", visible re-attempt on failure.
